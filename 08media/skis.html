Types of Skis

In this section, we'll break down each type of skis, from skis for groomed trails to those built for deep snow. Whether you're tackling steep slopes or icy terrain, knowing about ski design is crucial. Join us to learn about the various options, so you can pick the perfect ski for your style!

The main five skis we will review are as follows:

  1. Carving skis
  2. All-mountain skis
  3. Powder skis
  4. Freestyle skis
  5. Backcountry skis

Please find the definitions for each type of ski listed below:

Carving Skis-
designed for skiers who spend most of their time on easier terrain, making it easier to have smooth turns
All-Mountain Skis-
designed to preform on all types of terrain and snow conditions, including groomed, cruds, and moguls
Powder Skis-
wider and longer than normal skis, providing mroe control in powder and deep snow conditions\
Freestyle Skis-
used mostly for terrain park skiing, these skis are designed to be shorter and lighter with twin tips to allow for more balance and symmetry
Backcountry Skis
meant for runs outside of a ski areas boundaries, often require special equipment such as beacons and shovels
